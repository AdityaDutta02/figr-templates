Run better design critiques in 30 min.

A repeatable format that actually improves work — without the circular feedback loops.

The Problem

Most critiques are just opinion contests.

Without structure, design critiques devolve into whoever speaks loudest wins. Good work gets killed. Weak work ships. And designers leave feeling less confident than when they walked in.

DESIGN PROCESS
02 / 07 figr.design
1
Step 01 — 0–5 min

State the goal before anything else.

The designer presents in 60 seconds: what is this screen trying to do, and what specific question needs feedback? Everything said after that gets filtered through that lens — not personal taste.

FACILITATION
03 / 07 figr.design
2
Step 02 — 5–10 min

Silent review first. No talking.

Everyone looks at the design independently for 3 minutes and writes sticky notes — what works, what doesn't, what's unclear. This prevents anchoring bias where the loudest voice shapes everyone else's opinion.

BIAS REDUCTION
04 / 07 figr.design
3
Step 03 — 10–22 min

Feedback must reference the goal, not taste.

Enforce one rule: every critique starts with 'Given the goal of X…'. 'I don't like this font' is noise. 'Given the goal of quick scanning, this hierarchy makes the CTA hard to find' is signal.

FEEDBACK QUALITY
05 / 07 figr.design
4
Step 04 — 22–30 min

Designer closes with a decision, not a list.

The designer says what they'll change, what they'll ignore, and why — out loud, in the room. This closes the loop and signals to the team that critique is a tool, not a verdict.
